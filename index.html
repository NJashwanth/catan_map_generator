How to Use the Generator

  1. Pick the number of players. Games with 2–4 players use the classic 19-hex island; 5 and 6 players switch to the larger 30-hex extension layout with extra number tokens and ports.
  2. Optionally type a seed — any word or number. The same seed always produces the same board, so you can share it with your group (the URL also accepts ?seed=gamenight&players=6).
  3. Click Generate Map, or Shuffle Again to step to a fresh variation.
  4. Recreate the board with your physical tiles, number tokens, and harbor pieces — then play as usual.

What Makes a Board Fair?

Two dice follow a bell curve: 6 and 8 are rolled far more often than 2 or 12. When the high-probability tokens end up on adjacent hexes, whoever settles that corner gets a huge head start. With fair number spacing enabled, the generator rejects any layout where a 6 or 8 touches another 6 or 8, so strong spots are spread around the island.

Reduce resource clumping works the same way for terrain: it rejects boards where too many hexes of one resource sit side by side, so no single settlement can lock up all the wood or all the ore. Both checks can be turned off if you prefer fully wild boards.

Playing with 5 or 6 Players

The extension board adds roughly 50% more land: 30 terrain hexes, two deserts, 28 number tokens, and 11 harbors (five 3:1 harbors and six 2:1 resource harbors, including two sheep harbors). Games also use the special building phase: after each player's turn, everyone else may build with cards already in hand. Expect longer games — many groups raise the victory point target to 12 to keep the endgame from feeling sudden.

Reading the Number Tokens

The dots under each number show its probability out of 36 dice combinations — one dot for 2 or 12, up to five dots for 6 or 8 (shown in red). Adding up the dots around a settlement spot is the quickest way to compare starting positions.
