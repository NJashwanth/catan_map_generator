Frequently Asked Questions

Seeds, sharing, fairness rules, and everything else people ask.

How do seeds work?

Every board is produced by a deterministic random generator initialized from the seed text. The same seed with the same settings always produces the identical board — type gamenight today or next year and you'll get the same island. Leaving the seed blank uses the current time, so every click is fresh. The seed of the current board is always shown below the map, so you can write it down or share it.

Can I share a board with my group?

Yes — two ways. Share the seed and settings and let others type them in, or share a direct link: the site reads ?seed=… and ?players=… from the URL. For example, /?seed=gamenight&players=6 opens the six-player board for seed "gamenight" on any device.

What exactly do the fairness options do?

Fair number spacing rejects boards where a 6 or 8 sits next to another 6 or 8, so no corner of the island becomes overwhelmingly strong. Reduce resource clumping rejects boards where hexes of one terrain type bunch together. The generator shuffles and re-checks until a valid board appears. The board balance page explains the probability math behind both rules.

Why does the board say "fairness fallback"?

For a handful of seeds the generator can't find a layout satisfying every enabled check within its attempt limit, so it shows the best unconstrained board for that seed instead and labels it honestly. Shuffling again or tweaking the seed text virtually always resolves it.

Which player counts are supported?

Two through six. Games with 2–4 players use the classic 19-hex island with 9 harbors; choosing 5 or 6 players switches to the 30-hex extension layout with 28 number tokens and 11 harbors. The setup guide covers the differences in detail, including the special building phase used in big games.

Do I need to install anything?

No. The generator is a single lightweight page that runs entirely in your browser — phone, tablet, or laptop. Nothing you type is uploaded anywhere; board generation happens on your device.

Can I print a board?

The board is drawn as a crisp vector image, so your browser's print function (or a screenshot) reproduces it cleanly at any size. Landscape orientation fits the island best. Many groups simply prop a phone or tablet at the end of the table and copy the layout with physical tiles.

Is this an official Catan product?

No. This is an independent, fan-made setup utility for personal game nights. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to any publisher of the Catan board game, and all artwork on the site is original. You still need the physical game to play — this tool only decides where the tiles go.

I found a bug or have an idea.

Great — send it to jashwanthn066@gmail.com. Bug reports with the seed and player count that triggered the problem are especially easy to reproduce and fix.
